1) Postdoctoral position in Cancer Epigenetics/Immunobiology

The Micevic Lab is currently seeking a highly motivated postdoctoral researcher with a strong interest and experience in the fields of T-cell biology, immunology, and epigenetics. The group is broadly interested in cancer immunology with specific interests in identifying epigenetic mechanisms regulating CD8 T cell memory formation and exhaustion. Using mouse models, single cell approaches (scRNA-Seq, ATACseq), genetic perturbation tools (CRISPR) and clinical tissues samples, the group aims to lay the foundation for the next-generation of cancer immunotherapies. The position is based in New Haven, CT at Yale University, one of the world's leading locations for life sciences. Relevant background: https://www.pnas.org/doi/abs/10.1073/pnas.2304319120

  • Ph.D. in Molecular Biology/ Biochemistry, Immunology, Cancer immunobiology, genetics or related fields.
  • Demonstrated expertise (peer-reviewed publications) in the fields of immunology, cancer biology and/or epigenetics.
  • Experience in immunology techniques (flow cytometry, T-cell based assays, ELISA).
  • Proficient with molecular biology techniques (cell culture, RNA extraction etc. ).
  • Fluency in English, excellent written and communication skills.

  • Expertise using CRISPR-based approaches.
  • Trained for mouse work (handling, injections, tissue harvest).
